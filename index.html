About me

Florian Hofer is a researcher at the Free University of Bozen-Bolzano, Faculty of Engineering.

He holds a PhD in Computer Science from the Free University of Bozen-Bolzano (2022), with a thesis on "Resilience and Weaknesses of Smart Systems' Architectures".

His research focuses on enhancing the security and reliability of smart system architectures. He is particularly interested in real-time systems, industrial control systems, programmable logic controllers (IEC 61131-3), containerization and virtualization for Industry 4.0, cyber-physical systems, and IoT architectures including LoRaWAN and smart-lighting systems.

Research activities and interest

My research lies at the intersection of software engineering and hardware/systems engineering, focusing on the challenges posed by Industry 4.0 and smart system architectures. I am particularly interested in how software systems can be made more reliable, deterministic, and secure when operating in real-time and safety-critical environments.

Key research topics include: real-time systems and scheduling, industrial control systems and programmable logic controllers (PLCs / IEC 61131-3), containerization and virtualization for cyber-physical systems, smart-lighting and LoRaWAN/IoT architectures, and automated software testing for embedded and industrial systems.
